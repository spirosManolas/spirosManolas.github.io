About

I’m an undergraduate student at Stony Brook University studying Applied Mathematics and Statistics, with a strong interest in computational science, optimization, and interdisciplinary research. I’m currently supported by the Simons STEM Scholars Program, and I'm an active member of the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics, Spectra, and IEEE.

Research

During Summer 2025, I will be participating in a research project at Los Alamos National Laboratory, developing algorithms for contact and sliding in Arbitrary Lagrangian-Eulerian hydrodynamics simulations under the guidance of Nathan Vaughn-Kukura and Misha Shashkov.

In 2024, I participated in Emory University's Computational Mathematics for Data Science REU. I worked on generative methods for solving inverse problems in image reconstruction, developing a conditional continuous normalizing flow that outperformed conventional baselines such as FISTA.

As a research assistant in the CMADO Lab at Stony Brook, I’m working with Dr. Shikui Chen on the design of bistable devices for brain aneurysm treatment, using nonlinear topology optimization and conformal geometry.

I also assist in projects in quantum computing and quantitative finance with Dr. Hyun-Kyung Lim in the AMS Department. One such project explored quantum error mitigation strategies for the Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm (QAOA).

Field Experience

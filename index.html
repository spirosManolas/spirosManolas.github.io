Spiros Manolas

I am an aspiring computational scientist with research interests primarily in the development of algorithms for numerical methods and toplogy optimization with applications in the physical sciences and engineering fields. Currently, I am completing my undergraduate degree at Stony Brook University in Applied and Pure Mathematics with a minor in Mechanical Engineering as a member of the Simons STEM Scholars program. I am currently working under Dr. Shikui Chen in the Computational Modeling Analysis Design and Optimization (CMADO) Laboratory, where my work focuses on desinging bi-stable brain aneurysm implants through the development of a novel dual conformal geoemtry and non-linear topology optimization approach.

Previously, I worked with Dr. Hyun-Kyung Lim, under whom I explored projects relating to investigating quantum error mitigation for the Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm (QAOA) and parallelization for quantitative finance. In the summer of 2025, under the mentorship of Dr. Nathaniel Vaughn-Kukura and Dr. Mikhail Shashkov, I worked in Los Alamos National Laboratory's Computational Physics (XCP) Division on developing multi-velocity capabilities for arbitrary lagrangian eulerian contact algorithms. In the summer of 2024, under the mentorship of Dr. Nicole Yang, I participated in the Emory University REU on Computational Mathematics for Data Science where I completed a project on optimal experiment design and image reconstruction using generative methods with applications in medical imaging. In the winter of 2025, I participated in a research abroad experience at the Turkana Basin Institute (TBI) in Kenya, where I was able to develop a deeper understanding of the scientific process, sustainability efforts, and the importance of interdisciplinary research in addressing global challenges

Beyond my research, I am committed to making a positive impact on my community. I currently volunteer with the Sustainable Horizons Institute (SHI) , where I assist in the creation of "CULTIVATE Conversations," a series of online zoom talks and webinars for researchers of all levels with interests in STEM / the Computational Sciences, with the aim of building diverse and inclusive communities within the computational sciences. Additionally, I serve as the president of my university's Society of Industrial and Applied Mathematics (SIAM) Student Chapter, a Cohort Representative for the Simons STEM Scholars student government, and a Public Relations Officer for my university's American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA) Student Chapter.
